Father John Fu
(1903-2002)

- a tribute page

Fr. John B. Kaiping Fu was a Catholic priest, born in China before World War I and the Chinese Revolution, and who served in the Chicago area for the second half of his life. His experience as a theologian, educator, war and torture survivor, immigrant, polyglot, and community leader distinguish his life others.

Some key moments in his life include:

  • Born February 11, 1904, in Shandong Province in China.
  • 1916: Began training at Roman Catholic seminary.
  • 1930: Ordained as a Roman Catholic priest and went to Rome to study at Gregorian University.
  • 1934: Returned to China to serve as Dean of Students at Divine Word Fu Jen University, at the time in Peking/Beijing.
  • 1939: Accused of aiding students fleeing to South China during the Japanese Occupation of China. Arrested, imprisoned, and tortured.
  • 1948: Ordered by his superiors to study sociology in the United States, as Communism spread in China.
  • 1951: Began serving at Our Lady of Grace church in Chicago.
  • 1960s: Founded the Chinese Frienship House on West Fullerton Avenue on the north side of Chicago, whose mission was to support Chinese students coming to the area.
  • 1960s-1990s: Ministered to Chinese students at the Chinese Frienship House and the greater Chicagoland Chinese Catholic community. Said Mass, provided other Catholic sacraments, housed, and fed many people during this time.
  • 1998: Retired in Techny, Illinois.
  • Died February 11, 2002, at Divine Word Residence, in Techny, Illinois, on his birthday.
